Key Aspects of the AI Revolution

  1. Transformational Impact:

    AI is shifting from a tool for automation to an agentic, decision-making partner in sectors like healthcare, logistics, and finance.

  2. Infrastructure & Investment:

    Governments are investing heavily in GPU infrastructure, with India allocating ₹10,300 crore for AI computing power.

  3. Shift in Tech Roles:

    The focus is moving from writing code to orchestration and managing AI systems, necessitating new skills.

  4. Global Leaders:

    AI adoption is widespread, creating new economic opportunities and challenges, with leaders emphasizing responsible AI.

AI Boom

An AI boom is a period of rapid growth in artificial intelligence. The most recent boom began in the 2010s and accelerated in the 2020s, driven by generative AI and large language models.

History of AI

  1. In 1950, Alan Turing proposed the idea of thinking machines and introduced the Turing Test.
  2. In 1956, the Dartmouth Conference marked the official birth of AI as a field.
  3. In 1958, John McCarthy created LISP, a key programming language for AI research.
  4. In 1962, McCarthy founded the Stanford Artificial Intelligence Laboratory (SAIL).
  5. In 2011, Apple introduced Siri on the iPhone 4S, marking a mainstream AI assistant breakthrough.
  6. In 2022, ChatGPT launched and became the fastest-growing application in history.
